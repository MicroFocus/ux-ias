Side Navs

General

Side Navs work much like Nav Bars but are fixed to the side of a page instead of the top. Side Navs are used to provide users with a form of navigation within an application. They can be made to be toggleable, allowing a user to hide or show the Side Nav's content. Side Navs can make use of headings to separate listed items, as well as implement avatars to make list items more expressive.

Purpose

To provide users a toggleable navigation menu fixed to the side of a page.

Appearance

Common Patterns

  • Side Navs are fixed to the side of a page and allow another form of navigation to the user.
  • Side Navs can be made toggleable to either hide or show their content.
  • A smaller display would benefit from having a toggleable Side Nav, allowing more room for page content.
  • When a hidden Side Nav is toggled open, a dialog glass appears over the other page content to bring focus to the list of contents.
  • Side Navs can make use of section headings to separate content into meaningful sections to a user.
  • Avatar images can also be used within a Side Nav to give recognizable images and logos to listed items.

Nesting

Nestable Components
  • Buttons
  • Divs (Creates sections for avatar images)
  • Headers (Separate sections of Side Nav with descriptive header)
  • List items <li>
  • Spans
Nesting Side Navs in Other Components

Side Navs are a constant element that is fixed to the side of a page and is available throughout an application. Because of this, it is not likely a Side Nav would be nested within another element. In these situations, nesting a menu instead of a Side Nav will likely be more appropriate.

Responsive Display

Side Navs should be toggleable depending on the width of the current display. Larger displays will allow for a Side Nav to be open and fixed to the side of the window. As display width decreases, a Side Nav will become toggleable to allow more space for page content.
